Phase-Field Modeling and Simulation of Ferroelectric Domain Evolution

Home Projects

Overview

This project explores the application of phase-field modeling techniques to simulate the evolution of ferroelectric domains. Ferroelectric materials are widely used in memory devices, sensors, and actuators due to their unique polarization properties.

Objectives

  • Develop a phase-field simulation framework for modeling ferroelectric domain dynamics.
  • Analyze domain evolution under varying external conditions such as electric fields and mechanical stress.
  • Validate simulation results with experimental data.

Methods

The simulation framework employs the phase-field method, solving coupled partial differential equations that describe the domain structure and its evolution. Key tools and techniques include:

  • Finite-difference schemes for spatial discretization.
  • Time-stepping methods for temporal integration.
  • Visualization of domain patterns using VTK and ParaView.

Results

Preliminary simulations demonstrate the formation of complex domain structures and their response to external stimuli. Detailed results will be provided in subsequent updates.

Future Work

  • Incorporate additional physical effects, such as thermal fluctuations and defect dynamics.
  • Optimize computational performance for larger-scale simulations.
  • Collaborate with experimentalists to validate and refine the model.
