Training and Testing ¶

What is Training?

Training is the process where a machine learning model learns patterns from a given dataset. During this phase:

  • Input Data: The model is provided with features (independent variables) and their corresponding target labels (dependent variables).
  • Optimization: The model adjusts its internal parameters (like weights in a regression model) to minimize the error in predictions.
  • Output: The result is a trained model that has captured relationships in the training data.

What is Testing?

Testing evaluates the trained model's performance on new, unseen data to measure its generalization capability. This is essential to ensure the model isn't overfitting (memorizing training data) or underfitting (failing to capture key patterns).

Overfitting, Underfitting and Model Selection ¶

Overfitting occurs when a model learns the training data too well, including its noise and outliers. As a result, the model performs exceptionally on the training data but fails to generalize to new, unseen data.

Underfitting happens when a model is too simple to capture the underlying patterns in the data. This leads to poor performance on both the training and test sets.

It turns out that the test data, sometimes referred to as the "out of sample data", is a much better measure of how well your model performs in the real world. One reason for this is overfitting.

Let's go over some examples. It turns out these differences are more apparent in Multiple Linear Regression and Polynomial Regression so we will explore overfitting in that context.
